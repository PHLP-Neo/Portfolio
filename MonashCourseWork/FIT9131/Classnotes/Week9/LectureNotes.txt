Key concepts:
Design principles
 - Abstraction
  - In our assignment, for our dam class, we only add things that is important for our assignment
  - Waterlevel, release count in our class, not things like material or length.
 - Modularization
  - divide complex things into sub parts, like methods
 - encapsulation
  - grouping related fields/data and behavior/methods in a single unit.
 - information hiding
  - hiding and protecting fields from the outside world

Design Approach
 - Responsibility driven Design
  - design appraoach in OOP, what is going on in this class, what is this class for, encapsulation
  - in our Dam class, the dam only look after the dam, if the dam want to access the rainfall level,
  - it need to call the methods in the rainfall class.

Design Techinique:
 - Noun & Verbs

  - verb to get potential behaviors

Activity 1

Metropolitan bank offers the following types of accounts
to its customers: savings accounts, fixed deposit accounts, and
credit accounts. Customers may deposit money into an
account or withdraw money from an account. At the end of
each month interest is calculated for each account and
added to the account balance. Annual fees are charged to
the credit account and deducted from the balance.
